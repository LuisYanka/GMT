Seminars of Analysis and Geometric Measure Theory

Welcome to the webpage of Analysis and GMT!

Home

Welcome to the Seminars of Analysis and Geometric Measure Theory! This seminar series focuses on cutting-edge research in Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) and Geometric Measure Theory. PDEs are fundamental in modeling phenomena across physics, engineering, and biology, addressing problems like fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and wave propagation. Geometric Measure Theory provides tools to study complex geometric structures, such as minimal surfaces and rectifiable sets, with applications in optimization and material science.

This seminar is organized by Wei Wang and his colleagues at Peking University, bringing together researchers and students to explore seminal papers and recent advancements in these fields.

Spring 2025

In this seminar, we study some important papers in the research of geometric measure theory, analysis, PDEs, and the book "A Course in Minimal Surfaces" by Tobias Holck Colding and William P. Minicozzi (abbreviated as [CM11] in the following table). The reading seminar is held mostly every Thursday and Sunday afternoon, which starts from 14:00 at the Zhihua buildings of Peking University. Occasionally, due to the special schedules of the attendees, the time may change, and the seminar may be held online.

Attendees

Haotong Fu (Ph.D. candidate, PKU), Yuning Liu (Professor, NYU Shanghai), Huaijie Wang (Postdoctoral fellow, PKU), and Wei Wang (Ph.D. candidate, PKU).
